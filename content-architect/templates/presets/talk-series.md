# Talk Series Structure Preset

## Terminology
- **Sections** = Series Arcs (thematic groupings)
- **Units** = Individual Talks/Presentations
- **Opening** = Series Kickoff / Introduction Talk
- **Closing** = Series Finale / Wrap-up Talk

## Typical Structure

### Series Introduction
1. Series theme and vision
2. What the audience will experience
3. How talks connect to each other

### Talks
- 3-12 talks per series
- Each talk: 20-60 minutes
- Self-contained but connected by theme
- Total series: 2-12 weeks

### Series Wrap-up
1. Theme synthesis
2. Call to action
3. Next steps or follow-up series

## Architecture Patterns

### Sequential Narrative
Best for: story-driven series, theological journeys
- Each talk advances the narrative
- Cliffhangers and callbacks between talks
- Builds to a climax in the final talk

### Topical
Best for: survey series, theme exploration
- Each talk covers a different angle on the theme
- Can be consumed somewhat independently
- Connected by central question or concept

### Character/Case Study
Best for: biographical series, leadership lessons
- Each talk focuses on a different person, story, or case
- Common framework applied to each case
- Patterns emerge across the series

### Problem-to-Resolution
Best for: transformation series, coaching talks
- Early talks: Define and explore the problem
- Middle talks: Introduce framework and tools
- Final talks: Resolution and new reality

## Talk Template
1. **Hook** (story, question, provocative statement) - 2-3 min
2. **Context** (why this matters, connection to series) - 3-5 min
3. **Core message** (main teaching with 2-3 supporting points) - 15-30 min
4. **Application** (practical takeaway, exercise, challenge) - 5-10 min
5. **Bridge** (preview of next talk, maintain anticipation) - 2-3 min

## Duration Guidelines
- Talk: 20-60 minutes
- Series: 3-12 talks
- Series arc: 3-4 talks within a larger series
